Idea 1: The "Collocation Crusher" (Targeting the "Unnatural" Problem)
The Problem: They say "strong rain" (unnatural) instead of "heavy rain" (natural). Or "make an essay" (unnatural) instead of "write an essay" (natural).

The App Feature:

1. When a user saves a new word (e.g., "decision"), your app doesn't just save the definition. It also uses an AI-powered search to auto-populate common collocations.

2. The user's flashcard would look like this:

Word: Decision

Common Verbs: make a decision, reach a decision, reverse a decision

Common Adjectives: a big decision, a tough decision, a unanimous decision
3. The "Deep Study" Test: The app could then specifically test this. "Use 'decision' in a sentence with a verb other than 'make'."

Idea 2: The "Upgrade" Mode (Targeting the Passive-to-Active Gap)Paraphraze 
Advanced learners don't need to learn new words as much as they need to activate words they already know.

The Problem: The user writes, "This is a very big problem for our company." It's correct, but it's basic. They know the word "significant," but they lacked the confidence to use it.

The App Feature:

1. This is a new mode, separate from flashcards. Let's call it "The Upgrader."

2. The user writes a simple sentence: "This is a very big problem."

3. The user highlights "very big" and hits "Upgrade."

4. Your AI provides 3-4 alternatives with nuance:

"This is a significant problem..." (Formal, business)

"This is a huge problem..." (Strong, but still casual)

"This is a pressing problem..." (Implies it's urgent)

Why it's Better: This is a revolutionary way to study. It meets the learner at their current level and shows them how to upgrade their existing language using the passive vocabulary they already have.

Curious & Engaged: Instead of a boring grind, the "Upgrade" feature sparks curiosity. "I wonder what a better word for 'very big' is?" This turns learning from a repetitive chore into a creative act of discovery and refinement.

Empowered & Capable: When a user writes their own sentence in the "Deep Study" mode, they are moving from a passive consumer to an active creator. When the AI validates their sentence, the feeling is "I did it. I can use this word."

Confident: The "AI Naturalness Check" is the ultimate confidence-builder. It's a low-stakes, private "tutor" that answers their biggest hidden question: "Am I saying this right?" This confidence is what they need to take that word from the app into a real-world conversation.
